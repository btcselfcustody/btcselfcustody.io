Bitcoin Books

Below is a list of must-read books about Bitcoin and the disgrace of fiat money.

The Bitcoin Standard

The Bitcoin Standard examines Bitcoin's rise as a decentralized digital currency, comparing it to historical money systems like gold. It explores its economic, political, and social implications while addressing key questions about its technology and role in challenging government-controlled money.

The Price of Tomorrow

Jeff Booth argues that technological advances are deflationary and incompatible with outdated economic systems built on growth and inflation. He warns that relying on easy credit to drive growth creates unsustainable debt, and calls for a new economic framework that embraces deflation and abundance.

Broken Money

Broken Money explores the history of money through the lens of technology. Politics can affect things temporarily and locally, but technology is what drives things forward globally and permanently.

The Big Print

Most Americans , and people worldwide , understand that despite our remarkable technological advances, something is deeply wrong with the direction of our country and world. There are a variety of causes but Mr. Lepard believes, and wrote this book because, too many are missing the one, principal underlying cause: The Money Is Broken.
