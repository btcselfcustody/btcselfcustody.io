What is Self-Custody?â€‹

When you buy Bitcoin on an exchange, you don't actually own it yet. You own a promiseâ€”an IOU from a company that says they'll give you Bitcoin when you ask.

Self-custody means taking possession of your Bitcoin by controlling your own private keys. It's the difference between:

Company holds your BitcoinYou hold your Bitcoin
They can freeze your accountNo one can freeze your funds
You need their permission to withdrawYou don't need anyone's permission

Self-custody is how Bitcoin was designed to work. It's also a responsibilityâ€”there's no customer support, no password reset, and no reversing mistakes.

Self-custody means you are responsible for your Bitcoin. If you:

The Self-Custody Mindsetâ€‹

Before you dive into guides, internalize these principles:

1. Verify, Don't Trustâ€‹

Don't take anyone's word for itâ€”including this site. Verify addresses on your hardware wallet screen. Run your own node. Check multiple sources.

2. Move Slowlyâ€‹

There's no rush. A mistake with Bitcoin can be permanent. Read guides twice. Test with small amounts. Ask questions before acting.

3. Assume Compromiseâ€‹

Treat every device as potentially compromised. Air-gap when possible. Verify firmware. Don't trust, verify.

4. Separate Concernsâ€‹

Don't keep all your eggs in one basket. Geographic distribution. Different wallet types for different purposes. Backup redundancy.

5. Practice Recoveryâ€‹

A backup you've never tested isn't a backup. Verify your seed restores correctly before depositing significant funds.
